Privacy & Terms

No Attorney–Client Relationship. Submitting information through this site—including contact or intake forms—does not create an attorney–client relationship. Do not send confidential or time-sensitive information until we confirm representation in writing.

  • Analytics: de-identified/aggregate traffic metrics to understand how the site is used and to improve the experience.

How We Use Your Information

  • To respond to your inquiry, evaluate whether we can assist you, and communicate about next steps or scheduling.

We do not sell or rent personal information. Limited sharing may occur with service providers that help us operate the site (for example, website hosting, email delivery, or form routing). These providers may process data on our behalf under appropriate safeguards.

Form Routing & Third-Party Services

Form submissions may be transmitted through secure third-party platforms (e.g., workflow or email relay services) to route your inquiry to the firm. While we select reputable providers and apply reasonable measures, no system can be guaranteed 100% secure.

Cookies & Similar Technologies

This site may use cookies or comparable technologies for essential functionality and traffic measurement. You can adjust cookie preferences in your browser; disabling certain cookies may affect site features.

Data Retention

Inquiry records (e.g., contact form submissions) are typically retained for a reasonable period to respond and for routine business purposes. If we begin representation, your information will be governed by our engagement agreement and applicable professional obligations. If we do not represent you, we may delete or de-identify your submission in the ordinary course of business.

Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to the nature of the information we process. However, no method of electronic transmission or storage is completely secure.
